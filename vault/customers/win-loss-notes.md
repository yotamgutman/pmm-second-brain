# Win / Loss Notes

## Loss — Meridian Health Group (April 2026)

**Outcome:** Lost to [[opschain]]
**Reason:** Compliance team required a full audit log and field-level permissions for handling PHI-adjacent approval data. FlowPilot does not currently offer either — Audit Log is "in progress" per [[roadmap]] but not yet shipped. Price was not the deciding factor; OpsChain was actually the more expensive option.

## Win — Hearth & Co. (June 2026)

**Outcome:** Won, switched from [[routely]]
**Reason:** Hearth & Co. had outgrown Routely's manual CSV-only data movement and were spending several hours a week reconciling CRM data by hand. AutoSync was the deciding factor — see [[hearth-and-co]] for the post-sale check-in call where this came up again.

## Loss — Carrow Industrial (February 2026)

**Outcome:** Lost to [[opschain]]
**Reason:** Enterprise prospect, 400+ seats, required SOC 2 documentation for procurement. FlowPilot is SOC 2 Type I, with Type II in progress; OpsChain's existing Type II certification was a hard requirement for this deal.

## Win — Brightline Logistics (May 2026)

**Outcome:** Won, expanded from pilot to full rollout
**Reason:** The pilot team had been manually exporting/importing CSVs between FlowPilot and Salesforce every week — a pain point raised on the initial sales call (see [[brightline-logistics]]). The promise of AutoSync, in beta at the time, was a key factor in the expansion decision.

## Pattern Summary

Audit log and compliance documentation are the single most common loss reason against [[opschain]]. AutoSync has already converted one Routely win and influenced one expansion. Worth monitoring whether the Audit Log gap becomes more costly as FlowPilot moves upmarket.
